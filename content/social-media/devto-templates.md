## Pillar 4: Revenue-Based Financing Guide

### Template 4.1 - The Explainer
**Title:** Revenue-Based Financing: The Funding Option Most SaaS Founders Do Not Know About
**Body:** You receive a lump sum, repay a fixed percentage of monthly revenue, repayment adjusts with your revenue, total repayment is capped. No equity dilution. No personal guarantees. No board seats.

### Template 4.2 - The Comparison
**Title:** Equity vs Debt vs Revenue-Based Financing - A Founder's Decision Framework
**Body:** Equity is best for pre-revenue. Debt is best for established companies. RBF is best for recurring-revenue businesses that want to fund growth without dilution.
**Tags:** #startup #funding #founders #saas

### Template 4.3 - The Founder Story
**Title:** I Almost Gave Up 25% Equity Before Learning About RBF
**Body:** For SaaS companies doing $500K+ ARR with strong growth, RBF is often cheaper than equity. By the time you raise your Series A at 4x the valuation, that equity difference is worth millions.

### Template 4.4 - The Technical
**Title:** How to Model the True Cost of Revenue-Based Financing
**Body:** Effective cost = (Total repaid - Advance) / Advance. For high-growth SaaS, RBF is often cheaper than equity. For slow-growth, it can be more expensive than debt.
